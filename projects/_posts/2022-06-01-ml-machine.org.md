In order to let students explore how ML is embedded into our infrastructure, we designed [ml-machine.org](https://www.ml-machine.org), an educational tool for creating ML models with the [BBC micro:bit](https://www.microbit.org/), and incorporating them into interactive systems, thus making ML a design material. Through an in-situ pilot study in an 8th grade classroom we demonstrates that students were able to redesign everyday objects around the possibilities and limitations imposed by ML, but that they struggled to understand more advanced parts of ML such as data representation.

 <div class="img-container"><img class="full-width-img" src="/assets/images/projects/ml-machine/interface.jpeg" alt ="The ml-machine.org interface" /></div>

This tool explores how machine learning technologies can become a design material in the hands of students. By framing ML as a design material, we aim to specify that is is never a goal in it self, but is always incorporated into larger interactive systems which are imbued with intentions and goals. By letting students explore ML as a design material, we aim to support students in exploring the possibilities and limitations of the ML and code their own intentions into ML systems. We tested this tool in a pilot study in an 8th grade classroom in Denmark, a high-school classroom in California, USA and during the [Electromagnetic Field Camp](https://www.emfcamp.org) in the U.K.
