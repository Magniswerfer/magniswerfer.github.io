I am not my research. I am not just a researcher. I am priviliged. I am not objective. I am biased. I am willing to iterogate my privilige and my bias.

I will let me be defined by my research. I will be other things than a researcher. I will question my research.

My research aims to destabilise those in power. My research aims to empower those without it. My research aims to question existing power structures. My research suggests new ways of living. My research questions itself. My research questions me. My research is not objective.

---------------------------------

I was inspired to write the small manifesto after reading Josefin Wangel's excellent book chapter ''Troubling speculation'' from her, Eléonore Fauré and colleagues' excellent book ''Beyond Efficiency''. 

It is motivated by a feeling/fear I've had of being personally consumed by my research, and forgetting who I am, when I'm not a researcher / of being defined (or letting me define) as only my job. I is also equally motivated by my political believes and a belief that we are heading towards a technocratic dystopia at an alarming rate, and that our governing bodies (and "us" as individuals) are not equipped to deal with it. I would point you to the Netflix film "The Social Dilemma", which for all it's shortcomings (e.g., it's one-sidedness and eagerness to prescripe overtly nefarious motifs to developers of social media) does a good job of describing the situation we are currently in.

I hope/aim to make a difference.
